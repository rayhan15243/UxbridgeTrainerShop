Nike

Nike Air Huarache


Price: £80.00


We love the Nike Air Huarache OG. Tinker Hatfield's 1991 masterpiece is an offbeat classic, with a unique exoskeleton and neoprene sock fit and a distinct lack of swoosh that made it a high risk endeavour. After a Free-soled foray, the Huarache is back this season in a correct form (bar a bulkier forefoot than before) and these editions give us a dark grey suede and nylon combination that's one of the best makeups we've seen this year on this silhouette. Go at least half a size up on these for a good fit.

Nike Air Pegasus


Price: £75.00


The Nike Air Pegasus was 30 years old in 2013 and the Nike Air Pegasus 83/30 did a good job of upgrading iconic design language with some killer scope for colour that spreads from the upper to the sole unit via that Hyperfuse construction and angular midsole blocking. That tech jacket taping that unites the colours of mesh can't be beaten and this is one of the best updates of a shoe we've seen in a long time.

Adidas

Adidas Hamburg


Price: £70.00


This is a shoe for the connoisseurs. The adidas Hamburg was originally released in 1979 as a training design that was part of the fabled city series. Elevated to cult status by its rarely seen status and good looks, with the forefoot panel and ridged midsole giving it a distinctive look, now it's back as a 1:1 reissue in terms of shape, but special (and respectful) makeups like this tribute to the legendary adidas Brussel. Those that know will stack these up and they're the antidote to gimmicks and pointless technologies.

Adidas Forum Hi Og


Price: £90.00


The adidas Forum is a shoe that deserves to be treated with reverence. We like the low and we've seen more and more mids since the early 1990s, but this 1984 release's real majesty is in the high edition. The X-shaped panel, the angular Velcro fastening, the Dellinger web cushioning and built-in heel cup all made this the most expensive basketball shoe on the market for several years. Patrick Ewing wore 'em on the build up to his own line and we think that it might be one of the greatest shoes ever made. It's good to see it back in its original colours and towering height. Quality on this retro is on point.

Adidas Tobacco


Price: £65.00


The adidas Tobacco was originally released in 1978 with a casual leisure shoe simplicity that Euro connoisseurs went crazy for. We long for the simpler days when a sports brand could call a shoe Tobacco without causing an outcry. Now they're back in materials and colours that stick to the original Tobacco blueprint. These fit a little slim.
