Adidas

Adidas Gazelle


Price: £65.00


The adidas Gazelle is a design with enduring popularity with casuals, skaters, b-boys and blokes with big hats. This silhouette is tied with a handful of Trefoiled icons for the role of capo of the adi empire in terms of classic three striped shoes. In either its rounder OG shape or more streamlined sequels and minor remixes, you know the drill when it comes to this athletic creation. From the flat tongue to the distinctive reinforced toe, here's a creation that earned an appeal that's almost unanimous and isn't going anywhere soon in the timeless style stakes.

Adidas Consortium Clot X Adidas Stan Smith


Price: £63.00


After a brief time away it's good to see that the adidas Stan Smith is back. Introduced in 1971 as a slight remix of the mid 1960s Robert Haillet design, the pimple-sole, triple perforations and clean look made it one of the bestselling shoes ever. For 2014, this design has been slightly retooled for a superior shape and more comfortable fit. This special edition for adidas Consortium is reworked in association with Hong Kong's CLOT crew, with that heel tab and the black and gold premium makeover giving this some luxurious looks that are easy to wear.

Adidas Consortium Neighborhood X Adidas Stan Smith


Price: £63.00


The adidas Stan Smith is a classic that dates back to the early 1970s (though the Haillet actually dates back to the mid 1960s) and it's a shoe that's a right fit for Neighborhood's whole aesthetic. While all-white would be inappropriate for the NBHD worldview, we expected black and they've given us a brown boot leather, tan lining and some unique forefoot branding. With adidas's relationship with Shin and the team going way, way back (in fact the Superstar they collaborated on is nearly a decade old), this makes a lot of sense. Some might find that lettering a little odd, but it gives this shoe a new look that's steeped in nostalgia. With its retooled shape and improved fit, this adidas Consortium drop is a good addition to the collection.
